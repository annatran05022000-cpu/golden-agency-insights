## Start Here: The Non-Negotiables

Before any outreach is sent, make sure these three things are true:

**You know exactly who you're targeting**
ICP specificity is the single highest-leverage variable in outreach. "B2B companies" is not an ICP. "Owner-operated dental practices in Texas with 2-5 dentists" is an ICP. The more specific you are, the higher your reply rate will be — consistently.

**You have a working value proposition**
If you can't articulate the specific outcome your product produces for your specific ICP in one sentence, your messaging will be generic. Generic messaging is ignored.

**You have a place to track contacts**
HubSpot Free is sufficient. The requirement isn't sophistication — it's consistency. A CRM prevents duplicate outreach, tracks follow-up timing, and gives you data to optimize from.

## Second Step: Write Your Outreach Messages (Days 2-3)

### Message Structure and Architecture

A 7-touch outreach sequence that performs well follows this structure:

| Touch | Day | Goal | Tone |
|-------|-----|------|------|
| 1 | 0 | Establish relevance | Curious, not pitchy |
| 2 | 3 | Add value, restate | Helpful |
| 3 | 7 | Social proof | Credible |
| 4 | 10 | Different angle | Direct |
| 5 | 14 | Case study | Specific |
| 6 | 19 | Soft ask | Low friction |
| 7 | 25 | Breakup | Definitive |

### Your First Email (The Critical One)

Email 1 isn't trying to close anyone. It's trying to create enough interest to justify email 2.

Template structure:
- First line: Company-specific observation (not flattery — a real, specific detail)
- Lines 2-3: Link the observation to a real challenge or business opportunity
- Line 4: Single, low-friction CTA

What this looks like in practice:

---
*Subject: [Company]'s [recent thing]*

Hi [First Name],

[Company] seems to be [trigger observation]. Most [their title] I talk to at that point are dealing with [relevant challenge].

A few [similar company type] we've worked with have dealt with exactly this — [specific result they achieved]. Quick call to see if it's relevant?

[Your name]*

---

This is 68 words. Brevity signals respect for their time.

### The Most Critical Principle

Cold emails with personalized subject lines get 50% higher open rates, per [Yes Lifecycle Marketing](https://yesmarketing.com/resources/).

The subject line should include their company name or a specific reference to their situation — not just a {First Name} merge tag.

## Fourth Step: Launch and Monitor (Days 4-7)

### Days 1-2: Critical Setup

48 hours after your first send, review these key metrics:

**If open rate < 20%:** Your subject lines aren't working. Test 2-3 new variations.

**Good opens, low replies (under 1%):** Message body isn't landing. The opening gets them in, but the copy isn't compelling enough to reply.

**Replies OK, meetings low:** CTA friction is the problem. Positive responders aren't converting to meetings — lower the commitment in your ask.

**Bounce rate above 5%:** Data quality problem. Run your list through a verification tool, or switch to a source with built-in verification like **[GetLeadSnap](https://getleadsnap.pro)**.

### Your First-Week Dashboard

Set up these four numbers to check daily:

1. **Emails sent** (should match your target volume)
2. **Open rate** (target: 25%+)
3. **Reply rate** (target: 3%+)
4. **Meetings booked** — ultimately the conversion that matters most

## Fifth Step: Optimize from Data (Week 2+)

At the 200-300 contact mark with at least 3 touches each, the data becomes statistically useful for optimization.

**If open rate is low (below 20%):**
- Test new subject lines (5 variations minimum)
- Confirm send schedule is optimized: Tue-Thu, morning window (7-11am recipient time)
- Run a blacklist check on your sending domain to confirm deliverability status

**If reply rate is low (below 2%) despite good open rates:**
- Your first sentence isn't establishing relevance
- Your offer isn't specific enough
- Too much friction in the ask: 30-minute call vs. "quick question back?" or "worth a note?"

**If meetings are low despite good reply rates:**
- Your follow-up to replies is too slow
- Your scheduling process has friction (use Calendly)
- Replies are transitioning to calls but those calls aren't making it onto the calendar

## Avoiding the Most Expensive Mistakes

After reviewing dozens of B2B outreach programs, the same failure modes appear repeatedly.

### Failure Mode: Launching Without a Control Baseline

Teams that launch without a documented baseline have no way to know if changes are helping or hurting. After 3 months, they can't tell what's working.

Fix: Before any optimization, document your current open rate, reply rate, and meeting rate as a baseline. Changes only matter relative to that baseline.

### Failure Mode: Assuming Messaging Is the Problem

When results disappoint, the default reaction is to rewrite the email copy. In practice, copy is responsible for underperformance only about 30% of the time.

The more common culprits:
- **List quality** (accounts for ~40% of performance variation): Are you reaching the right people?
- **Timing** (accounts for ~15%): Are you sending at optimal times for your audience?
- **Sequence length** (accounts for ~15%): Are you following up enough times?

Fix: Eliminate list quality as a variable first. Use [GetLeadSnap](https://getleadsnap.pro) to ensure your contacts are current, verified, and ICP-matched before blaming copy.

### Failure Mode: No Systematic Prioritization

Without prioritization, every lead in your pipeline gets the same attention — which means your best prospects get the same follow-up as weak ones.

A simple tier system:
| Tier | Signal | Action |
|------|--------|--------|
| Hot | Opened 3x + clicked | Personal call or video message within 24 hours |
| Warm | Opened + clicked | Bump to front of sequence, personalized follow-up |
| Cold | Opened only | Standard sequence continuation |
| Inactive | No opens | Remove or re-engage with different angle after 30 days |

## What to Expect at Each Stage of Maturity

**0-60 Days: Calibration**

The goal isn't scale — it's signal. What ICP produces the best responses? What messaging angle gets replies? What follow-up timing works?

Measure: Reply rate per ICP segment, open rate by subject line variant. If you're seeing under 1% reply rate after 200+ contacts, stop and fix the ICP or message before adding volume.

**60-180 Days: Systematizing**

Convert what's working into documented process. Write down the ICP criteria, the sequence structure, the subject lines, the follow-up timing. This documentation is more valuable than any tool you could buy.

Measure: Meetings booked per 100 contacts sent. At this stage you want 1.5+ meetings per 100.

**180+ Days: Scaling**

Add contacts, add channels, add segments. The system is proven — now invest in reach.
